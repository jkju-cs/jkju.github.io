Our paper on the relationship between visualization literacy and visual attention patterns has been accepted to **IEEE VIS 2025** in Vienna, Austria!

## Research Overview

We used eye-tracking to study how people with different levels of visualization literacy allocate visual attention when interpreting charts. Our findings reveal systematic differences in fixation patterns that align with comprehension accuracy.

## Methodology

- 60 participants across three literacy levels (novice, intermediate, expert)
- 20 diverse chart types including bar, line, scatter, heatmap, and network graphs
- Eye-tracking + accuracy + confidence measures
- Qualitative interviews post-session

## Key Insights

Expert readers show more **efficient scanning patterns** and spend significantly more time on data-dense regions, while novices fixate on chart titles and legends. These differences are robust across chart types and suggest literacy-aware visualization design could meaningfully improve comprehension.
