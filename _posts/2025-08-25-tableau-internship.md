Reflecting on an incredible summer internship at **Tableau Research @ Salesforce**. Working with Srishti Palani and Arjun Srinivasan opened my eyes to the intersection of academic rigor and product-driven research.

## What I Worked On

My project focused on developing AI-assisted visualization recommendation systems — specifically, how to surface the "right" chart type to a user based on their data and analytical intent. I contributed to both the ML pipeline and the user evaluation studies.

## What I Learned

**Industry research moves differently.** The tight feedback loop with product teams means research questions are always grounded in real user needs. Hypotheses get validated (or invalidated) fast.

**Collaboration is everything.** Being embedded in a team with diverse backgrounds — engineers, designers, researchers — made the work richer. Every PR review and design critique was a learning opportunity.

**Your academic skills transfer.** The ability to design rigorous studies, analyze data carefully, and communicate findings clearly is genuinely valued in industry research.

## Would I Recommend It?

Absolutely. An industry research internship is one of the best ways to understand how your work can have real-world impact, and how academic research ecosystems differ from industry ones. Both have their strengths.

Thank you to everyone at Tableau Research for making the summer so memorable.
