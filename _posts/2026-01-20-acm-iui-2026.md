Thrilled to share that our paper on understanding how visualizations support decision-making and critical thinking has been accepted to **ACM IUI 2026**! We'll be presenting our findings in Paphos, Cyprus this spring.

## About the Paper

This work explores how interactive visualizations can scaffold decision-making processes for users with varying levels of expertise. We conducted a mixed-methods user study combining think-aloud protocols with behavioral logging to understand:

- How users navigate uncertainty in data
- What visual cues prompt deeper analytical thinking
- How visualization design affects confidence and accuracy of decisions

## Key Findings

Our results suggest that adaptive visual scaffolding — where the visualization adjusts complexity based on user behavior — significantly improves both decision quality and user confidence. Crucially, the benefit was most pronounced for users with intermediate visualization literacy.
